Who We Are

Our Foundation & Mission

Founded with the support of Afghan-Canadian residents in Ottawa and beyond, the Afghan Canadian Capital Society (ACCS) operates as a non-profit, non-political, and independent community association. Rooted in community service and cultural continuity, the society was established to preserve our language, culture, and values while active in building community opportunities in Canada.

Underpinned by a comprehensive, living framework of bylaws, ACCS maintains absolute dedication to transparency, fairness, and cooperative decision-making. We believe that the preservation of Afghan identity in the diaspora is made possible through collective effort, shared responsibility, and active civic participation.

Our Ethics

Core Values We Live By

ACCS is guided by five foundational ethics detailed in our bylaws, shaping all our programs, services, and interactions.

Cultural Expression

Safeguarding and promoting Afghan languages (Dari & Pashto), art, traditional music, and rich historical values to ensure continuity across generations.

Community Cooperation and Engagement

Fostering a network of mutual aid, supporting newcomer settlement, and providing help when it is most needed through collective responsibility.

Inclusivity & Respect

Welcoming all members of the Afghan community, respecting the absolute diversity of backgrounds, dialects, experiences, and beliefs in Canada.

Bridging Hearts & Generations

Weaving a cohesive social fabric by bringing youth, families, and elders together. Through cultural gatherings and collaborative initiatives, we dissolve barriers and build lifelong bonds, ensuring everyone feels a deep sense of belonging.

Active Volunteerism

Inspiring community members, youth, and elders to donate their time and skills, building local integration and giving back to Canadian society.
